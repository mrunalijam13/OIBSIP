DR.APJ ABDUL KALAM


(1931-FOREVER IN OUR HEARTS )




Journey of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam


Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist, visionary, and statesman. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam is widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to the development of India's space and missile technology.


Dr. Kalam had a humble background, growing up in a family of modest means. However, his determination, hard work, and love for science and technology paved the way for an extraordinary journey. He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College and later specialized in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.


Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam: Early Years of Brilliance

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Despite humble beginnings and financial challenges, he excelled in academics and developed a keen interest in science and aeronautics. These early experiences laid the foundation for his extraordinary journey as a renowned scientist and aerospace engineer.

Involvement in India's Missile Program

Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in India's missile development program. He was instrumental in the successful development of indigenous ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi, which bolstered India's defense capabilities.

Leadership at ISRO

Dr. Kalam served as the Director of the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) in the 1980s. Under his leadership, India successfully launched the satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and made significant strides in space technology.

Initiation of LCA Project

Dr. Kalam was a key figure in initiating the Light Combat Aircraft (LCA) project in 1983. The project aimed to develop India's first indigenous fighter aircraft, showcasing his commitment to indigenous technology and defense.

The Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests

As the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO), Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, demonstrating India's nuclear capabilities.

The People's President

Dr. Kalam was widely regarded as the "People's President" due to his approachable and humble nature. He remained connected with the citizens, especially the youth, and gained immense popularity and respect across the country.

Interaction with Students

Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam was passionate about inspiring and mentoring students. He extensively interacted with young minds, delivering lectures and encouraging them to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Vision for a Developed India

Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate for transforming India into a developed nation. He emphasized the importance of science, technology, and innovation to achieve sustainable economic growth and improve the quality of life for all citizens.

Authorship and Inspiration

Dr. Kalam authored several books, including the popular autobiography "Wings of Fire." His writings continue to inspire people worldwide, especially the younger generation, to pursue excellence and contribute to society.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam: An Inspirational Legacy

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. He suffered a cardiac arrest during the event and was pronounced dead at Bethany Hospital. His sudden demise deeply saddened the nation, but his inspirational legacy lives on.

"Lessons to be learn from APJ Abdul Kalam Sir"

  • Dream Big : Dr. Kalam always encouraged people to dream big and set ambitious goals for themselves. He believed that dreams fuel innovation and drive us to achieve great things.
  • Work Hard : Dr. Kalam's life is a testament to hard work and perseverance. He emphasized the importance of dedication and putting in the effort to turn dreams into reality.
  • Never Give Up : Dr. Kalam never gave up on his aspirations. He believed that failure is a stepping stone to success and taught us to keep moving forward, learning from mistakes.
  • Believe in Yourself : He encouraged people to have confidence in their abilities and to trust themselves to overcome challenges.
  • Education is Key : He believed that knowledge empowers individuals and societies, leading to progress and prosperity.
  • Stay Position and Optimistic : Dr. Kalam's infectious positivity and optimism were inspiring. He showed us that a positive attitude can transform challenges into opportunities.
  • Be Humble :He taught us the value of humility and respect for others.
